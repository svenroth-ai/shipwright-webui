The Command Center now drives from the keyboard. Press Ctrl+K (Cmd+K on a Mac) anywhere to open a search palette that jumps to a project, opens a screen, starts a task from that project's real actions, or filters the board — it floats as frosted glass over the backdrop, and the most recent commands come first. Press "?" for a cheat-sheet that shows every shortcut with both the Windows and the Mac keys side by side (Windows is the default, so a Mac-only hint never shows on Windows). In the board, projects, triage and inbox lists, "j" and "k" move a visible selection and Enter opens it; on the board list "L" launches the highlighted task. A comfortable/compact density control (in each list's header and in the palette) tightens the rows and is remembered across reloads. On a task screen, "t" jumps focus into the terminal and maximises it. Crucially, while you are typing in the terminal or any text box, every one of these shortcuts stands down — the letters you type reach Claude unchanged, exactly as before. Screens you have already loaded now repaint instantly from cache instead of flashing a spinner.
