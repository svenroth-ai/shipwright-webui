The Mission tab now shows you what a session is actually doing, live, without opening the terminal. Before, it only had something to show once a run had formally finished — any session that was still working, or an ad-hoc session, or the campaign orchestrator, just said "No run data yet — nothing to prove." Now the middle panel reads the session's own log and narrates it in plain language: a rolling "what's happening now" line ("Editing login.tsx", "Running tests", "Committing the change") and a short list of recent steps. The left panel leads with a plain "what this is" summary, shows where the work stands across four stages — Spec, Build, Test, Finalize — and folds the audit trail (requirement, spec, tests, review, commit) into clickable links that open on the right. When a run has genuinely finished it keeps its proof; when there is nothing in the log yet it says so honestly, and it never shows a stage it cannot actually work out (it shows a "—" instead). It only ever reads the log — it never writes to it — and the terminal is untouched.
