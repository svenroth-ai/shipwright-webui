The Files & Terminal view of a task now reads as three distinct cards floating on the shipyard backdrop: a glass Files card on the left, the Terminal/Transcript on a solid warm-beige "reading" card in the middle, and a glass Smart Preview on the right. Each card has a clearly-greyed header band, and the Transcript/Terminal switch and the file tabs now sit inside those bands as tidy segmented buttons. A new "Maximize terminal" button in the middle header collapses both side cards so the terminal fills the width, and clicking it again brings the side cards back — your saved panel widths are untouched. The terminal keeps its own dark screen inside the beige card, and — most importantly — nothing about how the terminal actually talks to your shell changed: the same keystrokes, pastes, and launch command reach it byte-for-byte as before. Resizing the panels, collapsing a side, switching tabs, and maximizing never restart the terminal session.
