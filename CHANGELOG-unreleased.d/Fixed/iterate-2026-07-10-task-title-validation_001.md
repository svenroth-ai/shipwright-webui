Creating or forking a task with an invalid title now fails cleanly instead of poisoning the task for every later launch (campaign webui-deep-audit D22, F27): POST `/tasks` and POST `/tasks/:id/fork` now validate a supplied title with the exact rule the rename (PATCH) endpoint already applies — embedded newlines (CR/LF/CRLF) and over-long titles are rejected up-front with a 400 and the same error message, so an automated writer (a run-config phase title, a triage "fix now" seed, or the leadwright daemon) can no longer persist a title that makes every subsequent Launch/Resume crash with a 500. A rejected fork no longer leaves an orphaned half-created child task behind, and an absent or blank title still falls back to the usual "Untitled task" / "<parent> — fork" default. The shared `normalizeTitle` helper makes create, fork, and rename share one validation contract.
