EmbeddedTerminal: atlas-corruption workaround for xterm.js 6.0 WebGL renderer (xterm.js#5847) now covers user-wheel scroll during streaming via a DOM `wheel` listener with 150 ms debounce (Tabby pattern from xterm.js#3864 / #3201). Together with the existing burst-after-quiet trigger, post-mount-settle backstop, conditional 10 s periodic, content-driven `onScroll`, and buffer-type-aware split (main-buffer: full `clearTextureAtlas` + `refresh`; alt-screen: `refresh`-only), smearing / ghosting / stale-cursor symptoms during sustained Claude TUI streaming are reduced from "visibly persistent until manual remount" to "max 10 s window, immediately resolved on scroll". Zero flicker when terminal is idle. Server-side `replay_snapshot` envelope additionally re-emits `?1006h` / `?1000h` / `?1002h` SGR mouse-encoding modes so mouse-wheel after detach correctly forwards to Claude instead of scrolling a frozen historical buffer (`@xterm/addon-serialize` does not serialize mouse-encoding modes).
